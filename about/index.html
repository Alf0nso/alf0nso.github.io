About Me

On the Internet I am generally known as Grasshopper, or “Gafanhoto” in Portuguese. I have already lived around age years on the planet earth and I hope to live the normal amount of time for a human being of my generation.

I’m currently on the second year of my master’s degree.

— Academic —

My Academic life started in Colégio de S.Tomás were I had my classes from 1st year of school to the last day of High School. During this period I took 3 Internships, (two related to biology and one to engineering), which left me confused with what to choose. Similar to the flip of a coin I decided to go for Geospatial/Geomatics engineering on Faculty of Sciences of the University of Lisbon. During this 4 years, the time it took me to finish my bachelor’s degree, I developed my taste for Engineering and Science topics. Close to the end of the third year I went to the Netherlands for an Internship on the ITC. After being back from Enschede I got another Internship on a company called Think Future.

Before the conclusion of my bachelor’s I was already certain that I wanted to pursuit a master’s degree, the uncertainty was present on my decision between Geodesy or Computer Science. Although I still maintain a fascination for the representation and measurement of the physical shape of the earth, I did choose CS as my master with the intention to have my thesis on Programming languages/Compilers.

— Music —

My garden of interests has quite a lot of species that never survived, but a couple ended up giving flowers. Music is by far the one I have sinked the most time and money, what started as casually learning an instrument for fun evolved into a couple of bands and musical projects that I hold dearly. I could easily spend quite a bit talking about each project but that would be more of a blog post, which this is not. For this reason I will just mention briefly that I played guitar on the song Nothing Concrete by Why Knot, played Drums and sang on The Spoon Of The Gods by Pod Pop and I am currently the Drummer of a band called Azma. I have to also mention that I was a student (4 years I think) on the International Drum Academy where I had classes with the great Michael Lauren.
